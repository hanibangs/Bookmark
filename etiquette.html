BOOKMARK ETIQUETTE

1. Avoid Folding Pages

Folding the corner of a page to mark your place can damage the book over time. Instead, use a bookmark to keep your place without causing any harm to the book.

2. Use Proper Placement

Insert the bookmark gently between the pages near the top or bottom of the page to avoid disrupting the text.

3. Avoid Heavy or Bulky Bookmarks

Using bookmarks that are too heavy or bulky can put unnecessary strain on the spine of the book. Opt for lightweight and slim bookmarks for best results.

4. Remove Before Closing

Always remember to remove the bookmark from the book before closing it to prevent any accidental damage or creasing.

5. Respect Library Books

If you're borrowing a book from a library, be extra careful with the bookmark. Avoid using sticky notes or anything that could leave residue or damage the pages.

6. Personalize Carefully

While it's fun to personalize your bookmarks with notes or decorations, be mindful of anything that could transfer ink or cause damage to the book.

7. Share with Caution

If you're borrowing a book from a friend or lending one of your own, make sure to remove any personal bookmarks or items before returning it.

8. Store Properly

When not in use, store your bookmarks in a dry, clean place to prevent them from getting bent or damaged.

9. Appreciate Bookmarks

Take the time to appreciate the bookmarks you use, whether they're simple or elaborate. They play an important role in preserving your reading experience.
