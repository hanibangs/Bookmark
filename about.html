WHAT IS A BOOKMARK?

PHYSICAL BOOKMARK

This refers to a tangible marker, often an elegantly designed piece of card, ribbon, or other material, inserted between the pages of a book. Its purpose is to denote the exact location where the reader paused their perusal, allowing for a seamless continuation of their literary journey. These bookmarks can range from simple, utilitarian designs to ornate, artistic creations that add an aesthetic touch to the reading experience.

DIGITAL BOOKMARK

In the realm of digital information, a bookmark serves as a refined method of saving the precise location within a document or web page. This enables users to effortlessly return to a specific point of interest without the need for laborious searching. In web browsers, digital bookmarks store the URLs of frequently visited or particularly significant websites, ensuring quick and easy access. Similarly, e-readers and PDF readers employ digital bookmarks to mark passages, allowing readers to navigate extensive texts with ease and precision.
